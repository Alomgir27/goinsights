================================================================================

WEEK 1: EVERYDAY ENGLISH BASICS
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Day 1
Title: 10 English Phrases You Use Wrong Every Day
Prompt: Create an educational video about 10 commonly misused English phrases. Include the wrong usage, correct usage, and real-life examples. Make it engaging with clear explanations.

Day 2
Title: Morning Routine Vocabulary - 50 Words You Need
Prompt: Create a vocabulary lesson about morning routine activities in English. Cover waking up, bathroom activities, breakfast, getting dressed, and leaving home. Include pronunciation tips.

Day 3
Title: How to Order Food in English Like a Native Speaker
Prompt: Create a practical English lesson about ordering food at restaurants. Include common phrases, how to ask for recommendations, dietary restrictions, and paying the bill.

Day 4
Title: 5 English Tenses Explained in 5 Minutes
Prompt: Create a quick grammar lesson explaining Present Simple, Present Continuous, Past Simple, Present Perfect, and Future Simple. Use everyday examples and keep it simple.

Day 5
Title: English Small Talk - Master Casual Conversations
Prompt: Create a lesson about small talk in English. Cover weather, weekend plans, work/school topics, and how to keep conversations going naturally.

Day 6
Title: 20 Phrasal Verbs Every English Learner Must Know
Prompt: Create an engaging lesson about essential phrasal verbs like get up, look for, give up, figure out, etc. Include meanings, examples, and common mistakes.

Day 7
Title: British vs American English - Key Differences
Prompt: Create a comparison video showing differences between British and American English in vocabulary, spelling, pronunciation, and expressions.

================================================================================

1. Keep videos 3-8 minutes for engagement
2. Use clear examples with on-screen text
3. Include practice exercises or quizzes
4. End with a call-to-action (subscribe, comment)
5. Add timestamps in description
6. Use consistent branding and intro/outro
